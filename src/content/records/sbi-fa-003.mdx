## OVERVIEW

Raid Guild is a decentralized collective providing design, development, and strategic services across web3 systems. It operates as a network of contributors coordinating through shared governance and reputation. The model enables flexible team formation without fixed organizational structure.

## SYSTEM COMPONENTS

**Member Network**
Distributed contributors with varied skill sets operating under shared coordination norms.

**DAO Governance Layer**
Proposal and voting systems guiding resource allocation and participation.

**Reputation Mechanisms**
On-chain and social signals used to evaluate contributors and form teams.

**Project Engagement Model**
Client work executed through ad hoc team assembly.

**Coordination Tooling**
Shared systems for communication, task management, and operational alignment.

## INTERPRETATION

Raid Guild explores organization design as an open system. Structure is minimal, with coordination emerging from incentives, reputation, and shared context. Teams form and dissolve in response to demand rather than hierarchy.

The model tests whether service organizations can operate without fixed roles or boundaries. It highlights both the flexibility and coordination overhead inherent in decentralized work.

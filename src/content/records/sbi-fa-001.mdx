## OVERVIEW

Superprism is an R&D environment focused on human-agent coordination. It develops systems where humans and AI agents generate and operate on shared context without centralizing control. The work targets scalable collaboration across mixed human and agent participants.

## SYSTEM COMPONENTS

**Collaborative AI Environments**
Shared operational spaces where humans and agents act on evolving context.

**Context Engineering**
Methods for structuring, maintaining, and isolating context across systems.

**Knowledge Interfaces**
Interfaces for navigating and modifying shared context.

**Multi-Agent Coordination**
Protocols for communication, task distribution, and coherence across agents.

**Agentic Economies**
Mechanisms for agent interaction within decentralized economic systems.

## INTERPRETATION

Superprism treats coordination as the primary constraint in applied AI systems. The focus shifts from individual model capability to shared context management across participants. Systems that cannot maintain coherent context degrade as agent count increases.

The work suggests that collaboration infrastructure will determine the usability of advanced AI. Context becomes the surface area where human judgment and agent execution meet.
